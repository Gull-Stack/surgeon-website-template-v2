Common Procedures Included

Breast Enhancement

Address changes in breast volume, shape, and position:

  • Breast Augmentation: Restore lost volume with implants
  • Breast Lift (Mastopexy): Elevate sagging breasts and reposition nipples
  • Augmentation with Lift: Both procedures for comprehensive enhancement
  • Breast Reduction: Reduce overly large breasts

Tummy Tuck (Abdominoplasty)

Often the cornerstone of a mommy makeover:

  • Removes loose, excess abdominal skin
  • Repairs separated abdominal muscles (diastasis recti)
  • Eliminates stubborn midsection fat
  • Removes stretch marks on the lower abdomen

Liposuction

Refine body contours and remove stubborn fat from flanks, hips, thighs, back, and arms.

Recovery Timeline

1

Week 1-2: Rest & Recover

Full-time help at home needed. Focus on rest, light walking, and prescribed medications. No lifting over 5-10 pounds.

2

Week 2-4: Light Activities

Return to light desk work. Gradually resume daily activities but avoid heavy lifting and exercise.

3

Week 4-8: Getting Active

Slowly return to normal activities and low-impact exercise. Most patients feel significantly better.

4

Month 3-6: Final Results

Swelling fully resolves. Scars continue to fade and mature. Final results become visible.

Are You a Good Candidate?

  • Completed your family (not planning future pregnancies)
  • At or near your ideal, stable body weight
  • Finished breastfeeding for at least 3-6 months
  • In good overall health
  • Non-smoker or willing to quit before and after surgery
  • Have realistic expectations
  • Have adequate help at home during recovery

Frequently Asked Questions

A mommy makeover is customized to each patient but typically includes breast enhancement (augmentation, lift, or both), tummy tuck, and liposuction. Additional procedures may include thigh lift, arm lift, or buttock enhancement.

Most patients take 2-3 weeks off work. Full recovery including strenuous exercise typically takes 6-8 weeks. Help at home, especially with young children, is essential during the first 1-2 weeks.

We recommend waiting at least 6-12 months after childbirth and 3-6 months after finishing breastfeeding. This allows your body to stabilize and ensures optimal results.

When performed by a board-certified plastic surgeon like Dr. Smith, a mommy makeover is safe. Combining procedures actually reduces overall anesthesia time compared to having separate surgeries.

While it's safe to become pregnant after a mommy makeover, future pregnancies may affect your results. We recommend completing your family before undergoing the procedure.
